Hayao Miyazaki: An Animation Career Spanning Over 40 Years

On 1 September 2013, Japanese animation legend Hayao Miyazaki announced that he would officially retire from making feature-length films. Join the South China Morning Post as we take a look at some of the most famous works in this legendary director's animation canon.

The Castle of Cagliostro

The first feature-length movie that Miyazaki directed, The Castle of Cagliostro used the character Lupin III, who also starred in a famous Japanese comic series and was inspired by Maurice Leblanc's Arsène Lupin, a self-proclaimed 'gentleman thief' similar to Sherlock Holmes.

March 11, 1984

Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind

Based on a seven volume comic series that Miyazaki wrote and illustrated from 1982 to 1994, this fantasy tale concerns a young princess named Nausicaä who must protect her homeland in the midst of a post-apocalyptic war. The story tackles themes of environmental protection and is seen as one of the biggest influences in Japanese animation.

Castle in the Sky

Castle in the Sky was the first film produced by the company that Miyazaki co-founded, Studio Ghibli, and tells the story of two adolescents caught up in the search for a mystical floating kingdom. The name of the kingdom, 'Laputa,' is a reference from Jonathan Swift's novel Gulliver's Travels, which inspired Miyazaki.
